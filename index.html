What is the Finance Bill?

Every year, Kenya's National Treasury tables a Finance Bill — a proposed law that changes how the government collects taxes. It's how Parliament decides what you pay more of, what gets cheaper, and what new taxes are introduced.

The Finance Bill 2026 (National Assembly Bill No. 26 of 2026) was released on 30 April 2026. If passed, most changes take effect on 1 July 2026. You have the right to comment before it becomes law.

Kiswahili

Muswada wa Fedha ni nini?

Kila mwaka, Wizara ya Fedha huleta Muswada wa Fedha — sheria inayopendekeza mabadiliko ya jinsi serikali inavyokusanya kodi. Ni njia ambayo Bunge hufanya maamuzi kuhusu nini kitaongezwa kodi, nini kitapunguzwa, na kodi gani mpya zitaanzishwa.

Muswada wa Fedha 2026 ulitolewa tarehe 30 Aprili 2026. Ukipitishwa, mabadiliko mengi yataanza kutumika tarehe 1 Julai 2026. Una haki ya kutoa maoni yako kabla haujawa sheria.

Key Impacts Explained

Athari Kuu § IMPACTS
📱

Mobile Phone Tax

Ushuru wa Simu

VAT on phones is removed — but a 25% excise duty kicks in when you first activate the phone on a network. Treasury argues this consolidates multiple existing taxes (VAT + Import fees + Railway levy). Critics warn it could raise prices for low-income buyers.

Before: ~55% combined taxes (import + VAT) → After: 25% excise at activation
🌐

Digital Money Transfers

Uhamisho wa Pesa – M-Pesa n.k.

Mobile money and digital payment services previously exempt from VAT will now attract 16% VAT. This includes M-Pesa transfers, payment gateways, and processing services. Could raise transaction costs for ordinary users and small businesses.

Before: Exempt → After: 16% VAT applied
🎬

Content Creator Tax

Kodi ya Wabuni wa Maudhui

If you earn money from YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, podcasting or any digital platform, 5% will be withheld from every payment you receive. The 2024 Bill proposed 1.5% — 2026's rate is more than three times higher.

Before: 0% → After: 5% withholding tax
🎰

Betting Winnings

Faida za Kamari

If you win from betting, sports betting, or gambling, the government will take 20% excise duty from your winnings — up significantly from the previous rate. Affects Kenya's large sports betting market.

Before: 7.5% → After: 20%
🏠

Rental Income Tax

Kodi ya Pango

The bill originally proposed raising the residential rental tax from 7.5% to 10%. Analysts warned landlords would pass this cost to tenants, worsening housing affordability in Nairobi and Mombasa. This proposal was later dropped.

Proposed: 7.5% → 10% — DROPPED ✓
🤝

Tax Amnesty Opportunity

Msamaha wa Madeni ya Kodi

If you have outstanding tax debts from periods up to December 2025, you can pay just the principal by December 31, 2026, and all fines and interest will be waived. This is an important opportunity to regularise your tax status.

Action: Pay principal by 31 Dec 2026 → penalties waived

Proposals Dropped After Public Pressure

Mapendekezo Yaliyoondolewa § DROPPED

The Finance Bill 2026 underwent revisions following public scrutiny. These proposals were withdrawn before Parliamentary vote — a reminder that civic engagement works.

👔

Mitumba Tax (Dropped)

Kodi ya Nguo za Mitumba — Imeondolewa

The 5% deemed profit tax on imported second-hand clothing (mitumba) would have raised prices in Kenya's massive second-hand clothing markets. The proposal was withdrawn after widespread criticism from traders and consumers who rely on mitumba for affordable clothing.

Status: DROPPED ✓ — nguo za mitumba hazitaathirika
🏘️

Rental Income Tax Hike (Dropped)

Ongezeko la Kodi ya Pango — Limeondolewa

The increase from 7.5% to 10% on residential rental income was dropped. Critics noted the higher rate had failed before (it was 10% from 2016–2023 and led to non-compliance). Analysts argued tenants would bear the cost through higher rents.

Status: DROPPED ✓ — inabaki 7.5%
🩸

Medical Supplies VAT Exemption Change (Revised)

VAT ya Dawa — Imerekebishwa

A proposal to exempt human blood and animal blood prepared for therapeutic use was flagged as procedurally inconsistent. The bill was revised to clarify VAT treatment of medical supplies, with animal blood-based products and dialysers receiving proper exemptions.

Status: REVISED ✓ — exemptions clarified

Frequently Asked Questions

Maswali Yanayoulizwa Mara Kwa Mara
Will my M-Pesa transactions become more expensive? ▼
Likely yes, unless the provision is amended. The Bill proposes to subject digital financial services — including money transfers, payment processing, and payment gateways — to 16% VAT. These services were previously exempt. The actual impact will depend on whether service providers absorb this cost or pass it to users.
🇰🇪 Kiswahili: Mapendekezo yanasema huduma za M-Pesa na uhamishaji wa pesa mtandaoni sasa zitatozwa VAT ya 16%. Hii inaweza kuongeza gharama za kuhamisha pesa.
Does the phone tax mean my new phone will cost 25% more? ▼
Not exactly. The 25% excise duty replaces several existing charges (16% VAT + Import Declaration Fee + Railway Development Levy). Treasury CS John Mbadi argues the combined old taxes totalled roughly 55%, so 25% is actually a reduction. However, critics note the shift from importation to activation creates new logistical and cost complexities. The net effect on retail prices remains disputed.
🇰🇪 Kiswahili: Serikali inasema ushuru mpya wa 25% unabadilisha kodi nyingi zilizokuwepo kabla (jumla ya ~55%). Wasiwasi upo kuhusu jinsi unavyoweza kuathiri bei za simu.
I earn from YouTube / TikTok. How does this affect me? ▼
If the Bill passes, 5% of every payment you receive from any digital platform (YouTube AdSense, TikTok, sponsored content, etc.) will be withheld before you receive it. This is a withholding tax, not an additional tax on top — it's collected at source and credited against your annual income tax. The concern is the rate: 2024's bill proposed 1.5%; 2026's is 5%, hitting emerging creators hardest.
🇰🍃 Kiswahili: Kama unapata pesa kutoka YouTube, TikTok au jukwaa lolote la kidijitali, 5% ya kila malipo itakatwa kabla ya kupokea. Hii ni changamoto kubwa kwa wabuniwa wachanga.
I sell mitumba. Do I have to pay the new tax? ▼
No — the mitumba tax proposal (5% deemed profit on imported second-hand clothing) was dropped following public pressure. Mitumba importers and traders are not subject to this new provision. However, you should continue to comply with existing import duties and income tax obligations.
🇰🇪 Kiswahili: Hapana — pendekezo la kodi ya mitumba limeondolewa. Wafanyabiashara wa mitumba hawataathirika na sheria hii mpya.
I have unpaid KRA taxes from previous years. What should I do? ▼
The Bill offers a tax amnesty: if your principal tax debt relates to periods up to December 31, 2025, and you pay the principal by December 31, 2026, all penalties and accumulated interest will be waived. This is a significant opportunity. Consult a tax advisor or contact KRA directly to assess your position.
🇰🇪 Kiswahili: Kuna msamaha wa kodi. Kama una deni la kodi kabla ya Desemba 2025 na ukilipa kiasi cha msingi kabla ya Desemba 2026, faini na riba zote zitafutwa.
Can I do anything to stop or change proposals I disagree with? ▼
Yes. The public participation process is real and has proven effective — the mitumba tax and rental tax hike were dropped after public feedback. You can submit a written memorandum to the National Assembly Finance Committee, attend public hearings in your county, petition your MP, or engage civil society groups. Act before the Bill is voted on in Parliament.
🇰🇪 Kiswahili: Ndiyo. Ushiriki wa umma unafanya kazi. Unaweza kuandika barua ya maoni kwa Kamati ya Fedha ya Bunge, kuhudhuria vikao vya umma, au kuwasiliana na Mbunge wako.

Glossary of Terms

Maana ya Maneno
Finance Bill
Muswada wa Fedha

Annual legislative proposal by Treasury to amend tax laws. Becomes the Finance Act once signed by the President.

VAT (Value Added Tax)
Kodi ya Thamani Ongezekaji

A tax charged at each stage of production/sale. In Kenya, the standard rate is 16%. You pay it on most goods and services.

Excise Duty
Ushuru wa Ziada

A tax on specific goods — like alcohol, tobacco, phones, and now betting winnings. Charged at manufacture or importation (or now, phone activation).

Withholding Tax
Kodi Inayokatwa Chanzo

Tax deducted before you receive payment. Your employer or platform takes it out automatically and pays KRA on your behalf.

KRA (Kenya Revenue Authority)
Mamlaka ya Mapato Kenya

The government body responsible for collecting taxes in Kenya. You file returns and pay taxes through KRA.

Capital Gains Tax (CGT)
Kodi ya Faida ya Uuzaji Mali

Tax on profit made when you sell an asset (land, shares) for more than you bought it for.

Tax Amnesty
Msamaha wa Kodi

A government programme allowing taxpayers to clear old debts by paying only the principal amount, with penalties and interest waived.

PPP (Public-Private Partnership)
Ubia wa Serikali na Sekta Binafsi

Projects jointly funded and managed by government and private companies — like toll roads, hospitals, or energy plants.

Agency Notice
Barua ya Makusanyo ya Kodi

A notice from KRA ordering a third party (e.g. your bank or employer) to pay your tax debt directly from your funds.
